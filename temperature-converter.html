Temperature Unit Description
Kelvin [K] The Kelvin scale is an absolute thermodynamic temperature scale where zero is absolute zero, the theoretical absence of all thermal energy. It is used in scientific research and in engineering.
Celsius [°C] The Celsius scale is a metric temperature scale where water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius and boils at 100 degrees Celsius at standard atmospheric pressure. It is used in most countries around the world for everyday purposes.
Fahrenheit [°F] The Fahrenheit scale is a temperature scale where water freezes at 32 degrees Fahrenheit and boils at 212 degrees Fahrenheit at standard atmospheric pressure. It is mainly used in the United States and a few other countries.

Temperature Units

Introduction: Temperature is a measure of the degree of hotness or coldness of an object or substance. It is an important physical property that is used in many different fields, from everyday life to scientific research. In order to measure temperature accurately and communicate temperature measurements effectively, a variety of temperature units have been developed. In this article, we will explore some of the most commonly used temperature units and their conversions.

Fahrenheit (°F)

The Fahrenheit scale is a temperature scale based on the freezing and boiling points of water. It was developed in the early 18th century by German physicist Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit. On the Fahrenheit scale, the freezing point of water is 32°F and the boiling point is 212°F. To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, use the formula: (°C × 9/5) + 32. To convert Fahrenheit to Celsius, use the formula: (°F - 32) × 5/9.

Celsius (°C)

The Celsius scale, also known as the centigrade scale, is a temperature scale based on the freezing and boiling points of water. It was developed in the mid-18th century by Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius. On the Celsius scale, the freezing point of water is 0°C and the boiling point is 100°C. To convert Fahrenheit to Celsius, use the formula: (°F - 32) × 5/9. To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, use the formula: (°C × 9/5) + 32.

Kelvin (K)

The Kelvin scale, also known as the absolute temperature scale, is a temperature scale based on the theoretical concept of absolute zero, the point at which all thermal motion ceases. It was developed in the mid-19th century by British physicist William Thomson, also known as Lord Kelvin. On the Kelvin scale, the freezing point of water is 273.15 K and the boiling point is 373.15 K. The Kelvin scale is often used in scientific research and engineering. To convert Celsius to Kelvin, simply add 273.15. To convert Kelvin to Celsius, simply subtract 273.15.

Rankine (°R)

The Rankine scale is a temperature scale based on the Fahrenheit scale, but with absolute zero as the zero point. It was developed in the mid-19th century by Scottish engineer William Rankine. On the Rankine scale, the freezing point of water is 491.67 °R and the boiling point is 671.67 °R. The Rankine scale is rarely used today, except in some specialized fields such as thermodynamics. To convert Celsius to Rankine, use the formula: (°C + 273.15) × 9/5 × 1.8. To convert Rankine to Celsius, use the formula: (°R - 491.67) × 5/9 - 273.15.

Conclusion

These are just a few of the many temperature units that are used today. Understanding temperature conversions is important for a variety of fields, from cooking to scientific research. By knowing the different temperature units and how to convert between them, you can communicate temperature measurements effectively and perform accurate calculations.

Temperature Formulas

Celsius to Fahrenheit Formula

The Celsius to Fahrenheit formula is a conversion formula used to convert temperatures from the Celsius scale to the Fahrenheit scale. The formula is:
F = (C x 1.8) + 32

In this formula, F represents the temperature in Fahrenheit and C represents the temperature in Celsius. To use the formula, simply multiply the temperature in Celsius by 1.8, then add 32 to the result to obtain the temperature in Fahrenheit.

Fahrenheit to Celsius Formula

The Fahrenheit to Celsius formula is used to convert temperatures from the Fahrenheit scale to the Celsius scale. The formula is:
C = (F - 32) ÷ 1.8

In this formula, C represents the temperature in Celsius and F represents the temperature in Fahrenheit. To use the formula, simply subtract 32 from the temperature in Fahrenheit, then divide the result by 1.8 to obtain the temperature in Celsius.

Celsius to Kelvin Formula

The Celsius to Kelvin formula is used to convert temperatures from the Celsius scale to the Kelvin scale. The formula is:
K = C + 273.15

In this formula, K represents the temperature in Kelvin and C represents the temperature in Celsius. To use the formula, simply add 273.15 to the temperature in Celsius to obtain the temperature in Kelvin.

Kelvin to Celsius Formula

The Kelvin to Celsius formula is used to convert temperatures from the Kelvin scale to the Celsius scale. The formula is:
C = K - 273.15

In this formula, C represents the temperature in Celsius and K represents the temperature in Kelvin. To use the formula, simply subtract 273.15 from the temperature in Kelvin to obtain the temperature in Celsius.

Fahrenheit to Kelvin Formula

The Fahrenheit to Kelvin formula is used to convert temperatures from the Fahrenheit scale to the Kelvin scale. The formula is:
K = (F + 459.67) ÷ 1.8

In this formula, K represents the temperature in Kelvin and F represents the temperature in Fahrenheit. To use the formula, simply add 459.67 to the temperature in Fahrenheit, then divide the result by 1.8 to obtain the temperature in Kelvin.

Kelvin to Fahrenheit Formula

The Kelvin to Fahrenheit formula is used to convert temperatures from the Kelvin scale to the Fahrenheit scale. The formula is:
F = (K x 1.8) - 459.67

In this formula, F represents the temperature in Fahrenheit and K represents the temperature in Kelvin. To use the formula, simply multiply the temperature in Kelvin by 1.8, then subtract 459.67 from the result to obtain the temperature in Fahrenheit.
